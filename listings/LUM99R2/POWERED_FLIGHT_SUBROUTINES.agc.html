Source Code

This is an attempt to recreate Luminary 99 Rev 2, otherwise known as LUM99R2, from Luminary 99 Rev 1 and ephemeris data from Luminary 116. The differences are confined to the last 10-12 lines of the CONTROLLED CONSTANTS. Luminary 99 Rev 1 source-code was transcribed from a printout in the MIT Museum, while Luminary 116 source code was transcribed from a printout belonging to Don Eyles. AGC developer James Kernan, the "rope mother" for the Apollo 11 Lunar Module's AGC, explains as follows: "At the last minute, Dan Lickly, our chief engineer, appeared with ephemerides updates and it took two tries to get it right. The result was that we created Lum99 Revision 1 and Lum99 Revision 2." It is suspected that the former is what's in the MIT Museum, and the latter is what we are trying to recreate here. As for which revision actually flew in Apollo 11, all of the other available surviving evidence points to Rev 1 rather than to Rev 2, so we're just not sure!

052730,000027:                                                                                                  #  CDUTRIG, CDUTRIG1, CDUTRIG2, AND CD*TR*GS ALL COMPUTE THE SINES AND
052731,000028:                                                                                                  #  COSINES OF THREE 2'S COMPLEMENT ANGLES AND PLACE THE RESULT, DOUBLE
052732,000029:                                                                                                  #  PRECISION, IN THE SAME ORDER AS THE INPUTS, AT SINCDU AND COSCDU.  AN
052733,000030:                                                                                                  #  ADDITIONAL OUTPUT IS THE 1'S COMPLEMENT ANGLES AT CDUSPOT.  THESE
052734,000031:                                                                                                  #  ROUTINES GO OUT OF THEIR WAY TO LEAVE THE MPAC AREA AS THEY FIND IT.
052735,000032:                                                                                                  #  EXCEPT FOR THE GENERALLY UNIMPORTANT MPAC +2.  THEY DIFFER ONLY IN
052736,000033:                                                                                                  #  WHERE THEY GET THE ANGLES, AND IN METHOD OF CALLING.
052737,000034: 
052738,000035:                                                                                                  #  CDUTRIG (AND CDUTRIG1, WHICH CAN BE CALLED IN BASIC) COMPUTE THE
052739,000036:                                                                                                  #  SINES AND COSINES FROM THE CURRENT CONTENTS OF THE CDU REGISTERS.
052740,000037:                                                                                                  #  THE CONTENTS OF CDUTEMP, ETC., ARE NOT TOUCHED SO THAT THEY MAY
052741,000038:                                                                                                  #  CONTINUE TO FORM A CONSISTENT SET WITH THE LATEST PIPA READINGS.
052742,000039: 
052743,000040:                                                                                                  #  CDUTRIG1 IS LIKE CDUTRIG EXCEPT THAT IT CAN BE CALLED IN BASIC.
052744,000041: 
052745,000042:                                                                                                  #  CD*TR*GS FINDS CDU VALUES IN CDUSPOT RATHER THAN IN CDUTEMP.  THIS
052746,000043:                                                                                                  #  ALLOWS USERS TO MAKE TRANSFORMATIONS USING ARBITRARY ANGLES, OR REAL

052748,000045:                                                                                                  #  NECESSARY IN PREPARATION FOR A CALL TO AX*SR*T IN EITHER OF ITS TWO
052749,000046:                                                                                                  #  MODES (SMNB OR NBSM).  SINCE AX*SR*T EXPECTS TO FIND THE SINES AND
052750,000047:                                                                                                  #  COSINES IN THE ORDER Y Z X THE ANGLES MUST HAVE BEEN PLACED IN CDUSPOT
052751,000048:                                                                                                  #  IN THIS ORDER.  CD*TR*GS NEED NOT BE REPEATED WHEN AX*SR*T IS CALLED
